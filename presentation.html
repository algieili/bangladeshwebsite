Discover Bangladesh

A Journey Through South Asia's Vibrant Heart

At a Glance

  • Independent since 1971 after a historic liberation struggle.
  • Home to 169M+ people - the 8th most populous nation.
  • Home to the Sundarbans, the world's largest mangrove forest.
  • One of the fastest-growing economies in the South Asian region.

The Land of Rivers

  • Situated at the junction of India and Myanmar.
  • Forms the world's largest river delta (Ganges-Brahmaputra).
  • Tropical Monsoon climate with three major seasons.
  • Rich fertile plains perfect for agriculture and jute.

Urban Centers

  • Dhaka: The mega-capital and historical trade hub.
  • Chittagong: Maritime gateway and commercial heart.
  • Khulna: Industrial city near the Sundarbans jungle.
  • Modern infrastructure bridging heritage with the future.

The Soul: Language

  • Bengali (Bangla): The 7th most spoken language globally.
  • The only nation founded on linguistic identity.
  • Rich multi-religious heritage: Islam, Hinduism, Buddhism, and more.
  • Known for hospitality and deep community bonds.

Vibrant Celebrations

  • Pohela Boishakh: Colorful Bengali New Year.
  • Eid-ul-Fitr: The major Islamic celebration.
  • Durga Puja: The grandest Hindu festival in the region.
  • Traditional music (Baul) and classical arts are daily life.

Flavors of Bengal

  • Staple: "Maach-e-Bhaat" (Fish and Rice).
  • Famous for Biryani and Spicy Hilsa Fish Curry.
  • Peerless dairy-based sweets like Rasmalai.
  • Tea culture: Bangladesh is a major global tea exporter.

History & Liberation

  • 1947: Emerged from the Partition of India.
  • 1952: The historic Language Movement.
  • 1971: Independence after the Liberation War.
  • A history defined by resilience and cultural pride.

Governance

  • System: Unitary Parliamentary Republic.
  • Sheikh Mujibur Rahman: The Father of the Nation.
  • Committed to secularism and democratic principles.
  • A key player in UN Peacekeeping operations globally.

Growth & Economy

  • Garments: 2nd largest RMG exporter in the world.
  • Agricultural powerhouse in Rice and Jute production.
  • Rapidly expanding IT and Digital Services sector.
  • Sustained 6%+ GDP growth over the last decade.

Tourist Paradise

  • Cox's Bazar: Longest natural beach (120km).
  • Sundarbans: UNESCO site and tiger habitat.
  • History: Mahasthangarh and Lalbagh Fort.
  • Green tea gardens in the hills of Sylhet.
