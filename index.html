Introduction

Bangladesh is a vibrant country located in South Asia. Known for its rich cultural traditions, beautiful natural landscapes, and rapidly growing economy, it emerged as an independent nation in 1971 and has since become one of the fastest-growing economies in Asia.

The country is famous for its intricate network of rivers, fertile agricultural land, vivid traditional festivals, and a massive, globally significant textile industry.

Bangladesh is also famously home to the Sundarbans, the largest mangrove forest in the world, which serves as the natural habitat of the endangered Royal Bengal tiger.

Land & Geography

Location

Situated in South Asia, bordered almost entirely by India on the west, north, and east, and by Myanmar on the southeast. The southern edge meets the majestic Bay of Bengal.

It forms a large part of the Ganges-Brahmaputra Delta, the largest river delta in the world.

Climate

A tropical monsoon climate with three main seasons:

  • Hot summer (March–June)
  • Rainy monsoon (June–October)
  • Cool winter (Nov–Feb)

Natural Resources

Rich resources power the nation's economy:

  • Natural Gas, Fertile soil, Fisheries, Timber, and Water.

Major Cities

Dhaka

The capital and largest city. The political and cultural heart.

Chittagong

The main seaport and center for trade.

Khulna

Industrial city near the Sundarbans.

People & Culture

Language & Religion

Bengali (Bangla) is the official language. Major religions include Islam, Hinduism, Buddhism, and Christianity.

Festivals

Pohela Boishakh: New Year. Eid al-Fitr: Muslim festival. Durga Puja: Hindu festival.

Food & Cuisine

Famous for flavors like Biryani, Hilsa Curry, and Panta Bhat.

Traditional Clothing

Sari: Worn by women. Panjabi: Worn by men during festivals.

History & Governance

Partition of India

Initial formation as East Pakistan.

1971 Liberation War

Triumphant independence after a nine-month war.

Political System

A parliamentary democratic government guaranteeing citizens’ fundamental rights.

Key Leaders

Sheikh Mujibur Rahman: Founding father. Sheikh Hasina: Longest-serving leader.

Economy & Tourism

Major Industries

Textile & Garment, Agriculture, Shipbuilding, IT, and Fisheries.

Key Exports

Ready-made garments, Jute, Seafood, and Leather goods.

Tourism

Visit the world's longest beach at Cox's Bazar and the historic Lalbagh Fort.
